Hidden Gem

Double Decker Root Bridge

Meghalaya

Ancient living root bridges woven by the Khasi tribe. These bio-engineering marvels are unique to Meghalaya and grow stronger with time. A perfect destination for eco-tourism and trekking enthusiasts.

Ziro Valley

Arunachal Pradesh

Home to the Apatani tribe and the famous Ziro Music Festival. This UNESCO World Heritage candidate offers lush pine hills, rice fields, and a unique cultural experience that captivates every visitor.

Hidden Gem

Loktak Lake

Manipur

The world's only floating national park, Keibul Lamjao, is located here. Famous for its phumdis (floating biomass) and the endangered Sangai deer, it's a surreal landscape that must be seen to be believed.

Majuli Island

Assam

The largest river island in the world, renowned for its Satras (Vaishnavite monasteries) and vibrant culture. A hub of art, music, and spirituality nestled in the mighty Brahmaputra river.

UNESCO Site

Kaziranga National Park

Assam

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for hosting two-thirds of the world's great one-horned rhinoceroses. The park offers jeep and elephant safaris through its tall grasslands and tropical forests.

Tawang Monastery

Arunachal Pradesh

The largest monastery in India and the second largest in the world, perched at 10,000 feet. It is a significant center of Buddhist learning and culture, offering breathtaking views of the Tawang Chu valley.

Shillong

Meghalaya

Known as the "Scotland of the East" for its rolling hills and colonial legacy. Famous for its waterfalls, music festivals, and the vibrant Police Bazar. A cultural hub of the Northeast.

Gangtok

Sikkim

The capital of Sikkim, offering stunning views of Mt. Kanchenjunga. A cosmopolitan hill station blending tradition and modernity, famous for its monasteries and the MG Marg promenade.
